Willpattu National Park

Wilpattu, Sri Lanka's largest national park, spans 130,000 hectares and is the oldest, reopened after years of closure due to armed conflict. The park is known for its leopard population and large sand-rimmed lakes, known as "Villus," which attract wildlife during drought. The main attractions include leopards, sloth bears, and barking deer. Wilpattu is also known for its archaeological and historical significance, with archaeological ruins and stories about the Sinhalese kingdom 500 years before Christ's birth.

Willpattu National Park's Diversity

Wilpattu, Sri Lanka's largest national park, spans over 130,000 hectares and is named after floodplain lakes called 'villus'. The park boasts a diverse flora and fauna, including over 30 mammal species and wetland bird species. Originally a wildlife sanctuary, it was designated a National Park in 1938. During the Civil War, it was a popular park, but was shut down in 1985 due to LTTE attacks. It reopened in 2010 and welcomes visitors.

Wilpattu National Park Diversity Facts

  • 1. Flora Variety: Wilpattu boasts a diverse range of plant species, including rare and endemic flora, contributing to its rich botanical tapestry.
  • 2. Abundant Fauna: The park is home to a myriad of wildlife, from the elusive Sri Lankan Leopard to elephants, sloth bears, and numerous bird species, offering a comprehensive safari experience.
  • 3. Unique Ecosystems: Wilpattu features a mosaic of ecosystems, including dense forests, expansive grasslands, and serene water bodies, creating a habitat for various species adapted to different environments.
  • 4. Willus (Natural Lakes): The park is dotted with "Willus," natural lakes that serve as crucial watering holes for wildlife, attracting a diverse array of animals throughout the year.

The Terrain Explained

Wilpattu National Park, Sri Lanka's largest wildlife sanctuary, spans 131,693 hectares and features a unique complex of over 50 wetlands called "Villu." These shallow natural lakes, filled with rainwater, are surrounded by open grassy plains and dense scrub jungle. The park experiences a dry zone, with the main rainy season occurring from September to December and the inter-monsoon season from March to April.

Key Features of Wilpattu's Terrain

  • 1. Diverse Landscapes: Wilpattu showcases a diverse terrain, encompassing dense forests, open grasslands, and captivating water bodies.
  • 2. Coastal Beauty: The park features a coastal stretch, providing a unique blend of terrestrial and marine landscapes.
  • 3. Expansive Willus: Natural lakes known as "Willus" dot the terrain, serving as vital watering holes for the park's wildlife.
  • 4. Sand-rimmed Water Holes: The park's landscape is adorned with water holes surrounded by sandy rims, creating picturesque scenes and essential resources for animals.

Conservation & Guidelines

At the heart of Wilpattu National Park lies a steadfast commitment to conservation, and your visit plays a pivotal role in preserving its natural allure. Amidst the thriving biodiversity, you have the unique opportunity to actively contribute to sustainability. Discover how you can help safeguard the delicate balance of this ecosystem, ensuring that Wilpattu remains a thriving sanctuary for generations to come. As you explore this remarkable park, abide by these guidelines to harmoniously coexist with the natural wonders that call Wilpattu home.

Conservation Initiatives:

  • 1. Habitat Preservation: Wilpattu focuses on protecting diverse ecosystems to ensure the thriving of its unique flora and fauna.
  • 2. Anti-Poaching Measures: Rigorous efforts are in place to safeguard wildlife, with a particular emphasis on protecting the iconic Sri Lankan Leopard.
  • 3. Community Engagement: Wilpattu actively involves local communities to promote sustainability and contribute to wildlife protection.

Visitor Guidelines:

  • 1. Stick to Trails: Explore the park responsibly by sticking to designated paths, minimizing impact on vegetation and habitats.
  • 2. Respect Wildlife Distances: Ensure the safety and well-being of animals by maintaining a respectful distance during your exploration.
  • 3. Dispose of Waste Properly: Keep Wilpattu clean by carrying out waste responsibly, respecting the park's pristine environment.
  • 4. Follow Park Regulations: Adhere to guidelines provided by park authorities to contribute to a positive impact on Wilpattu's conservation efforts.

Embrace these guidelines to actively participate in Wilpattu's commitment to preserving its natural splendor.
